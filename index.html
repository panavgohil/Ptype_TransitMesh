Protocol Guide

How does AetherNet work?

AetherNet is a decentralized mobile communication tool designed to function without internet, cellular data, or central routers.

It utilizes a "Store-and-Forward" architecture where messages physically hop between devices using Android's short-range radio hardware. We call this Epidemic Routing: when nodes meet, they replicate messages, using a Time-to-Live (TTL) limit to prevent storage saturation.

Will this drain my battery?

No, thanks to BLE Triggering. Constant Wi-Fi scanning normally drains batteries in under 4 hours.

AetherNet keeps the high-power Wi-Fi Direct radio completely off. Instead, it relies on Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) for peer discovery, waking up the Wi-Fi socket connection only when a peer is in range to transfer payloads.

Why run in the background?

Modern operating systems aggressively kill background apps to save memory. AetherNet runs as a Foreground Service.

This elevates its priority in the Android scheduler, preventing the OS from terminating it, ensuring the shadow network remains active even when your phone is locked.

What about connection delays?

Establishing a Wi-Fi Direct connection can take 10-15 seconds. To prevent failed handshakes, we have implemented an Optimized Handshake algorithm.

The app only attempts a connection if the BLE signal strength (RSSI) is stronger than -70dBm, ensuring devices are physically close enough for a stable, high-speed link.
